Metal Fabrications: Kinetics is a semester-long Techincal Arts course offered at Lick-Wilmerding High School. I took this class during the fall semester of my junior year at LWHS (Lick-Wilmerding). In the class, we learned about pneumatics, hydraulics, and simple machines like levers and pulleys. This class mainly focused on the mechanics of motion, looking at concepts like gears, cams, levers, and linkages, and taught me how I could use these to create mechanical advantage. Not only did I learn a lot, but I was also able to have a lot of freedom with many of the choices I made - I chose what materials to work with, what concepts I wish to use, and what final project I wanted to complete. For every project, I was required to design and create each assigment while following a schedule that I assigned myself. Because this course is so focused on kinetics and motion, I was able to apply what I learned in my physics course into the designing phase of my assignments.
