Metal Fabrications: Kinetics is a semester-long Techincal Arts course offered at Lick-Wilmerding High School. I took this class during the fall semester of my junior year at LWHS (Lick-Wilmerding). In the class, we learned about pneumatics, hydraulics, and simple machines like levers and pulleys. This class mainly focused on the mechanics of motion, looking at concepts like gears, cams, levers, and linkages, and taught me how I could use these to create mechanical advantage. Not only did I learn a lot, but I was also able to have a lot of freedom with many of the choices I made - I chose what materials to work with, what concepts I wish to use, and what final project I wanted to complete. For every project, I was required to design and create each assigment while following a schedule that I assigned myself. Because this course is so focused on kinetics and motion, I was able to apply what I learned in my physics course into the designing phase of my assignments.

Projects:


    For our pinewood derby car project, we were put into pairs to make the fastest derby car in the class. My partner and I decided to create a Pinewood Derby from a block of redwood, dowel, and MDF.

    We spent one class researching designs and watching videos where other people made derby cars and talked about what made a derby car fast. Using what we learned, we spent to rest of the class sketching out rough drafts and ideas.The next class, we shared and picked an idea and began building. We spent the next two classes cutting, sanding, and putting our derby car together. Next class, we raced our derby cars. Though our derby car did not win, it placed 3rd.

    For pneumatics, hydralics, and linkages projects, my partner and I were prompted to create a machine that would go up a metal ramp and knock down a pile of blocks. We spent two classes designing and researching and five classes building and testing.

    Overall, our project and time management was pretty successful. We worked our best to be efficient and get as much done so that in case something broke, we would still have extra time to finish it. We were also pretty fair when it came to distributing the work; for example, while I was designing things on rhino, Marc would be cutting out pieces of wood. We would also constantly be checking in with each other to know what we should do next. I feel like we could have spent a little more time designing and prototyping; we spent one class designing and even then, it was not a very well thought out design. The design did not take into account how inclined the slope was and it didn’t really consider all the different factors; we just chose the design because we believed it would be the one that would most likely succeed at the task. We didn’t really plan a lot, we just jumped into it. Once we chose a design and somewhat determined the final idea, we just gave ourselves a task and once we finished, we worked on the next one till it was done. I think that we were really efficient at making our design. We were also smart in picking a design that would not be too difficult to make and not have much workload, but I think we fell short in our actual designing process. Because we didn’t spend time thinking about all the different factors and scenarios that could hinder our success, it was not very well-designed and had a hard time making it up the ramp.

    For our project, we chose to use linkages and pneumatics. We used a bell crank which can change the angel of motion (input, output) to move the wheels. We then used pneumatics to move linkages and to lift the machine up and down to reset the syringe that will move the linkage.

    Summary of Project

    For our final projects, we were tasked to create anything we wanted as long as we applied one of the following materials we learned from class. I decided to create a kinetic sculpture that used gears. It would also have multiple CNC parts (laser cut), and be motor-driven. I used MDF, clear acrylic, acrylic dowell, and a motor.
